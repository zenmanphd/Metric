No place like home

American Pride

Why should we adopt some fussy new measurements that would only complicate our rich American way of life and “inch” us dangerously close to the snare of global conformity? We hard-working Americans are far too busy setting olympic world records, brewing craft beer, and setting an example for the world to crowd our minds with pointless meters, liters, and grams. Besides, why should we re-invent the wheel when so much of this great nation has already been built with American made, U.S. customary units (previously the British imperial system). The American people do not need a math lesson in decimal conversion. Everyone here intuitively knows what 3 feet looks like. We can easily comprehend a distance of 30 miles, because the speed limit on the interstate tells us that if we are driving at a pace of 60 miles per hour, then it will take us 30 minutes to get there. The police in any town can recognize a suspect as being 5ft 7in tall because generations of Americans dating back to the original 13 colonies have described height in this way. Our conventional units don’t just influence how we measure things, but how we think, communicate, and interact with each other.


But perhaps more Americans could be persuaded to embrace metric or S.I (Système International d’Unités) units if they had a proper handle of traditional measurements. Sometimes it can be helpful to have a quick, accurate understanding of length, volume, distance when a tape measure isn't handy. You may find it to cultivate an accurate reliable sense of scale for making educated guesses on the fly until a more accurate method can be obtained later.
